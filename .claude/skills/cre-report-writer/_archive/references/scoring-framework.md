# Scoring Framework - What Makes a "Top Opportunity" for Calusa

A project ranks high when MULTIPLE of these are true; no single factor suffices on its own.

## Stage (most important - drives the financing window)

- `Planned / Permitting` → **HIGHEST.** Construction debt/equity not yet placed; live window.
- `Under Construction` → second. Bridge, mezz, gap, refi/takeout still viable.
- `On Hold / Stalled` → third. Recap or rescue-capital play.
- `Completed` → **LOWEST.** Relationship intel only, not a lead.

For news-only opportunities, infer stage from article language: default to Planned/Permitting
for plans/announcements, Under Construction once groundbreaking has occurred. This ordering is
the deliberate **INVERSE** of a generic "completed-first" instinct - financing-advisory
mandates are won before the capital stack closes. Never normalize it.

## Sponsor Profile

**IDEAL TARGETS - rank HIGHEST even at smaller deal sizes:**
- Local Florida developers - single-market operators (Fort Myers, Naples, Punta Gorda,
  Sarasota/Bradenton/Lakewood Ranch radius). Most accessible.
- Regional Florida-focused firms not on the national institutional-advisor circuit.
- Newer sponsors - first/second project, family operators, owner-user-to-developer transitions.
- Single-purpose LLCs whose principals trace to a local/regional operator - do the lookup
  rather than dismissing as anonymous.

**WORTH PURSUING but flag the competitive context:**
- Established multi-asset / institutional developers (Lutgert, TRG, Seagate, Stock, Kolter,
  Greystar, similar). Track and capture, but note in the Fit that they likely already hold
  institutional advisor relationships.

**LOW PRIORITY:** religious organizations, public-sector entities, hospital systems.

## Deal Size

- $3M - $30M → **sweet spot, highest priority.**
- $30M - $100M → worth pursuing but flag bulge-bracket competition.
- $100M+ → generally too large for Calusa; capture as intel only.
- Under $3M → relationship play only.

## Asset Class

- **HIGH FIT:** Industrial (distribution, small-bay, IOS, flex/spec), Luxury Auto Storage,
  Multifamily, Mixed-Use, Land.
- **MEDIUM-HIGH:** Retail (grocery-anchored, strip), Office.
- **MIXED FIT:** Self-Storage (non-luxury-auto), Hospitality, Senior Living,
  Medical/Healthcare (MOB sponsorship only).
- **LOW FIT:** Religious, Government, single-tenant institutional, hospital systems directly.

## Contact Actionability

- Named principal + direct phone → ready to call.
- General company line only → research before calling.
- No contact info → research first, then decide.

## News-Signal Boost (merged-universe logic)

- A pipeline record with one or more recent linked news articles is a STRONGER signal than the
  same record without coverage - active press means capital decisions are happening now. Rank
  these higher within the same stage/profile/size tier.
- A standalone news article (no `Linked Pipeline Record`) is a fresh lead the permit scanner
  hasn't caught yet. Treat it as a pipeline record; use its `Financing Relevance Score` as a
  starting input, then apply this full framework.
- A "High" news score does not auto-promote to Top Opportunity - re-score it here. The news
  scanner's score is a triage signal, not a substitute for this framework.

## Judgment Overrides

- A small ($1-3M) project from an ideal-target sponsor (local, newer, high-fit asset class) can
  still be a Top Opportunity for relationship reasons.
- A $50M+ project from an unknown LLC with no contact is NOT a Top Opportunity until researched
  - flag for Sunbiz/web research instead.
- An institutional sponsor inside the sweet-spot size range is worth surfacing as a Top
  Opportunity if there is any credible differentiation angle.
